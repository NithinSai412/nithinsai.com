Why You Should Be Eating Oats!?

Benefits Of Eating Oats

Oats are a type of cereal grain that has been a staple of human diet from many years. They come in many forms. Rolled Oats and Stell-Cut Oats are some of the different types of oats. Starting of your day with the right breakfast is very important and there is no better way to start than eating oatmeal as your first meal of the day. Lets look at benefits of eating oatmeal in the article below

Very Nutritious

When it comes to healthy breakfast options, oatmeal surely comes up as one of the best choices. They are a great source of carbs and fiber and also a source for high quality protein, with a good balance of essential amino acids. A 50g of oats contains 180 calories and 8g of protein. It makes you feel for longer and helps you stay fit!. Oats contain energy, protein and fat, in addition to dietary fiber, B-vitamins, iron, sodium, manganese and calcium among others. Also, oats contain zero cholesterol.

They Can Lower Cholesterol Levels

High blood cholesterol is a major risk factor for heart diseases. Heart diseases are one of the leading causes of death for adults. Beta-Glucan fiber that is available in oats is shown be effective in reducing LDL(bad) cholesterol levels in many studies. When the LDL cholesterol reacts with free radicals, oxidation of LDL cholesterol occurs which is a critical step in progression of heart diseases. The antioxidants in Oats work together with vitamin C to prevent LDL oxidation and thus reducing risk of heart diseases.

Diabetes Control

Studies have showed that oatmeal consumption decreased the blood sugar level significantly in tested population. One reason this was attributed to was that a high fiber diet and complex carbohydrates took a longer time to digest than simple carbohydrates. This helps in maintaining optimum blood sugar levels. In the study, an oatmeal diet was given to patients in the replacement of other cereals and grain.

Anticancer Properties

Colon cancer is dreadful and can be excruciatingly painful. One study, conducted by researchers in the United Kingdom and the Netherlands, found a link between people who ate a high fiber diet (primarily from whole grains and cereals like oats) and a reduced risk of colorectal cancer. According to the study, every additional 10 grams of fiber in a person's diet reduces their risk of developing colorectal cancer by 10%!

Some other benefits include

  • Weight Loss
  • Reduced Constipation
  • Asthma Prevention
  • Immunity Benefits

How To Prepare Oatmeal

  • Step-1:Take a cooking vessel and add some water (or 200ml-300ml milk) and bring it to boil.

  • Step-2: Now take a cup of oats(40g-50g) and combine with water(or milk). Stir the oats and cook on a medium heat for one minute.

  • Step-3:Turn off the flame and let the oatmeal cool down for sometime

  • Step-4:Serve with toppings, such as fruits(such as apple, banana or pomegranate),nuts and dried fruits

Summary

Oats are an incredibly nutritious packed with important vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. In addition, they're higher in fiber and protein compared to other grains.Benefits of having oatmeal include lower blood sugar and cholesterol levels, protection against skin irritation, and reduced constipation.Moreover, they are very filling and have many properties that should make them a food helpful for weight loss.At the end of the day, oats are among the nutrient-dense foods you can eat.
