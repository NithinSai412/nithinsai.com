Best Places To Visit In Hyderabad

Hyderabad- The City Of Many Names

Covering over 650 sq. Kilometers, Hyderabad was estabilshed by Quli Qutb Shah in the year 1591 on the banks of Moosi river that orginates in the Ananthagiri hills. The 4th most populous city in India, This metro city houses over 10 million people. Hyderabad is a city that has people belonging to various religions and cultural backgrounds live here. This diverse city has many beautiful places you can visit. This city is a place worth visiting at least once in a lifetime as there are many tourism palces to visit in Hyderabad, from families, young peope, children to senior citizens. In fact, it is impossible for anyone to soak in the charms which the city has to offer in a limited time span. Check out this article to about these places.

Charminar

The most populor tourist attraction of telangana and Hyderabad is Charminar. Built in the year 1591 by Quli Qutb Shah the founder of Hyderabad. Standing at 160ft tall this structure is beautiful to visit in the evenings.

Golconda Fort

Just 11 km away from the city, the architectural marvel Golconda Fort is well connected to the rest of the city. Built by Qutub Shahi Kings, this fort presents an impressive structure, with eight gates and 87 bastions.

Chowmahalla Palace

Chowmahalla Palace, with literal meaning of ‘four palace's in Urdu language was built in the 18th century. It was during the reign of fifth Nizam, Afzar-ud-Daulah, Asaf Jav V, from 1857 to 1869, that the palace was completed.

Hussain Sagar Lake

Connecting the twin cities, Hussain Sagar Lake boasts of being the largest artificial lake in Asia. On the tributary of Musi River, it was excavated during Ibrahim Quli Qutub Shah's reign, in 1562 AD. It has got its name after Hussain Shah Wali.

Qutub Shahi Tombs

Constructed with grey granite, these quadrangular structure with dome shape roofs are ornamented with beautiful designs. The garden ‘Ibrahim Bagh’ which surrounds the tombs is popular as picnic spots.

Birla Mandir

Made from 2000 tonnes of marbel this temple has 11 ft. tall statue of Lord Lakshminarayana with a magnificent lotus carved around it. It also has separate shrines for deities such as Venkateswara, 'Padmavati' and 'Andal' as well.

Salar Jung Museum

At the banks of Musi River, Salar Jung Museum is located. With an impressive collection of 43000 art objects, 9000 manuscripts and 47000 printed books, it is considered to be one of the biggest one-man collections of the world.

Sanghi Temple

About 25 km away from Hyderabad, Sanghi Temple is located on the hillock Paramanand Giri. Dedicated to the Lord Venkateshwara, the architecture of this temple is based on the Chola-Chalukya architectural style.

Nehru Zoo Park

Located near the near Mir Alam Tank of Hyderabad with a vast area of 380 acres. Boasting of about 1,500 species of birds, animals and reptiles this largest zoo in India is run by the Forest department, Government of Andhra Pradesh.

Mecca Masjid

In 1617 it was built by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutub Shah, although the construction was completed in 1694 by the Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb. With a height of almost 75 feet and dimensions of 220 feet by 180 feet, it can easily accommodate about 10,000 devotees.

Birla Planitorium

This planetarium offers four sections, each offering a unique opportunity to explore the unknown. Interactive science centre, Window on Science, Archaeology Center and the Nirmala Birla Art Gallery and Dinosaurium are the four section of the planetarium.
